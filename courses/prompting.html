Core Prompting Techniques

Master these fundamental patterns that work across all AI models

📝

Zero-Shot Prompting

Beginner

Direct instructions without examples. The foundation of all prompting—clear, specific requests that guide the model.

Summarize this article in 3 bullet points,
focusing on key business implications.
📚

Few-Shot Learning

Few-Shot Tutorial → Beginner

Provide examples that demonstrate the desired format, style, or reasoning pattern you want the model to follow.

Convert to formal:
"gonna be late" → "I will be delayed"
"stuff's broken" → "The system is malfunctioning"
"dunno" → ?
🔗

Chain of Thought

CoT Tutorial → Intermediate

Instruct the model to think step-by-step. Dramatically improves accuracy on complex reasoning and math problems.

Solve this step by step:
If a train travels 120 miles in 2 hours,
then stops for 30 minutes, then travels
80 more miles in 1 hour, what's the
average speed for the entire journey?
🎭

Role Prompting

Intermediate

Assign the model a specific persona or expertise. Shapes response style, depth, and domain knowledge.

You are a senior security engineer
with 15 years of experience in
penetration testing. Review this
code for vulnerabilities.
🌳

Tree of Thoughts

Advanced

Explore multiple reasoning paths simultaneously. The model evaluates different approaches before committing to a solution.

Consider 3 different approaches to
solve this problem. For each approach:
1. Outline the strategy
2. Identify pros and cons
3. Rate likelihood of success (1-10)
Then choose the best approach.
🔄

Self-Consistency

Advanced

Generate multiple responses and select the most consistent answer. Improves reliability on complex tasks.

Solve this problem 3 different ways.
Then compare your answers. If they
differ, analyze why and provide
your most confident final answer.
🔍

ReAct (Reasoning + Acting)

Advanced

Interleave reasoning traces with actions. The model thinks, acts, observes, and adjusts in a loop.

Thought: I need to find the latest
         revenue figures
Action: search("company Q3 revenue")
Observation: [search results]
Thought: Now I can calculate...
📋

Structured Output

Intermediate

Define exact output formats using JSON, XML, or markdown schemas. Essential for programmatic consumption.

Extract entities and return as JSON:
{
  "people": ["name1", "name2"],
  "organizations": ["org1"],
  "dates": ["YYYY-MM-DD"]
}

Common Prompting Mistakes

Learn what NOT to do to improve your prompts

❌

Being Too Vague

Bad "Write something about marketing"
Better "Write a 500-word LinkedIn post about B2B SaaS marketing trends in 2025, targeting CMOs"
❌

No Output Format

Bad "List the pros and cons"
Better "List 5 pros and 5 cons in two columns. Keep each point to one sentence."
❌

Overloading Single Prompts

Bad "Analyze this data, create visualizations, write a report, and suggest next steps"
Better Break into 4 separate prompts, each focused on one task
❌

Negative Instructions Only

Bad "Don't be boring, don't use jargon, don't be too long"
Better "Be engaging, use simple language, keep it under 200 words"
